Atoms & Charges

Everything is made of atoms with protons (+), neutrons, and electrons (-). Normally, atoms have equal + and - charges, making them neutral.

üß§

Rubbing Transfers Electrons

When you rub the balloon on the sweater, electrons jump from wool to rubber. The balloon gains extra electrons (becomes -) and the sweater loses electrons (becomes +).

üß≤

Opposite Charges Attract

The fundamental rule: + attracts - and same charges repel. This is why the charged balloon sticks to the sweater and can pick up small pieces of paper!

üè†

Induced Charge on Wall

When the - balloon approaches the wall, it pushes wall's electrons away, leaving + charges near the surface. This creates attraction even though the wall was neutral!

‚ö°

Static Discharge

When enough charge builds up, electrons can jump through air as a spark! This is what happens with lightning - a giant static discharge between clouds and ground.

üî¨

Triboelectric Series

Different materials have different tendencies to gain or lose electrons. Rubber loves to grab electrons, while wool easily loses them. That's why this combination works so well!

‚ö° Fun Facts

1

A single lightning bolt can contain up to 1 billion volts of electricity! That's enough static electricity to power a 100W light bulb for 3 months.

2

You experience static shocks more in winter because dry air is a poor conductor. Humidity helps discharge static electricity safely.

3

Helicopters use grounding wires to discharge static before people exit. Without this, the static buildup from rotating blades could cause dangerous sparks!
